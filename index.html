The Rise and Fall of the Compact Disc in the Music Industry

Project Overview

The purpose of this project was to analyze how the music industry has changed over the last few decades. To accomplish this analysis, we first pulled data from data.world website to assemble a dataset of over 3000 data points.

Through this analysis we wanted to observe the changes that have occured in the music industry in the last few decades, what kind of music formats have become popular, which music format has become outdated and where is the future of the music industry heading.

Business Case

The benefit of this project would be for any budding new artist to understand the music market. It would be great learning for marketing firms to visualize how to market their client's music. It will help them to analyze the end user consumtion market. It will provide answers to questions like Which format is the best way to release music so that it maximizes record sales.

It would save a lot of money for marketing and music production companies if they understand how and in what form to release the music which would be easily consumed by the general public. This analysis leads the way in understanding that.

Tableau Visualizations

Animated Music Format Ranking Bar Chart (1973-2019)

This chart shows us how the landscape for the music comsuption has evolved since 1973. As you see in the animation the Cassette format came into picture in the late 1980s while the CD format dominated the industry from the 1990s till the 2014 when the internet took over the market.

It is fascinating to observe in this animated graph that how the CD format took over the music market by storm and dominated it for nearly 15 years up until as recently as 2014.

Music Sales chart

This bar chart provides a visual representation of the sales for each format for each year. Here we can can see how each format has fared in comparison to each other.

An important observation that can be made in this graph is that the Cassette format which had great sales from 1980 to 1990 is completely non existent a few years after that.

Percentage of Market share for each format

This bubble chart shows the percentage market share occupied by each format over the period from 1973 to 2019. Here we can observe just how dominant the CD format of music was as it held a 95% share of the market at one point of time.

However, with the introduction of Ipods at around 2004 the CD market share started declining and currently stands at 5% of the market as of 2019. This also shows the meteoric rise of the 'Paid Subscription' format which currently sits at more than 50% share of the market and looks primed to grow further in the coming years and be the music format of the future.

Conclusion

With this Music Data Analysis we can conclude that the CD format of music dominated the industry for almost 20 years. At this present time however with the introduction of the paid subscription format and the cloud seems to be the future of Music. Applications such as Spotify, Deezer and others have finally started taking over the market and hence the CD is at the brink of extinction.

Hence any new artist who tends to release the music would be wise to stick with the online, cloud based format of marketing and selling music
